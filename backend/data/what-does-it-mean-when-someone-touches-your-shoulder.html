The Dominant Touch

A dominant touch is a physical gesture that is often employed to assert authority or control, establishing a power dynamic rather than fostering a sense of connection or mutual understanding. This type of touch can be executed in a forceful or assertive manner, sending a clear message of dominance. For example, patting someone on the shoulder or tapping them in a commanding way can be used to get their attention or signal them to finish a task or leave a situation. In these cases, the dominant touch is less about establishing a connection and more about asserting control or authority.

However, it is important to note that a dominant touch can also be used as a gesture of appreciation, particularly in a hierarchical setting. For instance, a superior may pat a subordinate on the shoulder as a way to acknowledge their efforts and assert their dominance. This type of touch can make the recipient feel good and validate their work.

On the other hand, it is crucial to recognize that dominant touches can be inappropriate, especially if they are used in an unequal or unfair manner. Unequal shoulder patting, for example, can indicate favoritism in the workplace, creating discomfort and tension among co-workers. It is essential for individuals to be mindful of the power dynamics involved in dominant touches and to ensure that they are used appropriately and respectfully.
